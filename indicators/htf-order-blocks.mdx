## Overview

**HTF Order Blocks** detects and draws institutional order blocks from multiple higher timeframes and overlays them on your current chart. An order block is the last bearish candle before a bullish impulsive move, or the last bullish candle before a bearish impulsive move — representing the price level where institutional orders were originally placed. These zones frequently attract price on retests.

The indicator detects order blocks across up to six configurable timeframes (current timeframe + five HTF slots) using ATR-based sensitivity filtering to ensure only significant blocks are drawn. When an order block is violated — price closes through it in the opposite direction — it can optionally convert to a breaker block, which is then drawn in a lighter color to indicate the flip.

Each OB is drawn as a labeled box with the timeframe and direction noted. A volume bar inside the box (optional) shows relative volume at the time the OB formed. The indicator is built for multi-timeframe confluence: when a current-timeframe entry setup aligns with an HTF OB, it significantly increases the probability of the zone holding.

## Settings

### General

**OB Detection Sensitivity** — `High` | `Medium` | `Low` (default: `Medium`)

ATR-based filter for order block size. `High` (0.2× ATR) catches most blocks. `Medium` (0.5× ATR) filters small ones. `Low` (1.0× ATR) only shows large, significant blocks. Adjust based on how many blocks you're comfortable tracking.

---

**Maximum Lookback** — `1 Day` | `1 Week` | `1 Month` | `Max` (default: `1 Week`)

How far back order blocks are sourced. Older blocks are typically less relevant intraday; use `1 Week` for clean charts.

---

**Show Breaker Blocks** — toggle (default: off)

When on, OBs that have been violated (price closes through them) are converted to breaker blocks and continue to display in a lighter shade. Disable if you only want to see active, unmitigated OBs.

---

### Timeframes

**Show Current Timeframe** — toggle (default: on)

Enables order block detection on the chart's current timeframe.

---

**Show HTF 1 through HTF 5** — toggle + timeframe selector (each)

Five independently configurable higher timeframe slots. Each can be enabled or disabled and set to any timeframe. Defaults are blank (auto-select based on current chart timeframe).

---

### Appearance

**Bearish OB Color** — color picker (default: red, 80% transparent)

Fill for bearish order blocks.

---

**Bullish OB Color** — color picker (default: teal, 80% transparent)

Fill for bullish order blocks.

---

**Bearish Breaker Color** — color picker (default: red, 40% transparent)

Fill for bearish breaker blocks (more opaque than OBs to differentiate).

---

**Bullish Breaker Color** — color picker (default: teal, 40% transparent)

Fill for bullish breaker blocks.

---

**Label Color** — color picker (default: white)

Color of the text label inside each OB box.

---

**Label Size** — `Tiny` | `Small` | `Normal` (default: `Small`)

Size of the label text.

---

**Show Volume** — toggle (default: off)

Displays a small volume bar inside each OB box showing relative volume at formation. Useful for judging the institutional significance of the block.

## How We Use It

HTF Order Blocks are the team's primary supply and demand reference beyond FVGs. When looking for entries, the team checks whether the target pullback zone is also an HTF OB — when a 15-minute FVG overlaps with an HTF OB from the 1-hour or 4-hour chart, the confluence is treated as the highest-quality entry location on the chart.

The breaker block feature is used selectively — primarily on higher timeframes like the 4-hour or daily, where a broken OB creates a meaningful flip zone. For intraday 1-minute execution, breakers are less relevant than fresh OBs and FVGs.
