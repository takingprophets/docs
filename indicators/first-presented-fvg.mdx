## Overview

**First Presented FVG** is built around a specific ICT concept: the first Fair Value Gap that forms in the AM session (between 9:32–10:00 AM ET) and PM session (between 1:32–2:00 PM ET) carries special significance. These gaps mark the initial directional bias expressed by the market at the start of each session — where institutions began their delivery. Price frequently returns to the first gap before continuing or reversing, making it a reliable intraday draw target and re-entry zone.

This version of the indicator shows exactly one gap per session per day — the single gap that opened each session's delivery. It resets at 9:30 AM each day when "Only show current day gaps" is enabled. The indicator detects both bullish and bearish first-presented gaps automatically, and it includes a full **Opening Range Gap (ORG)** overlay — the gap between the previous day's 4:14 PM close and the 9:30 AM open, with gradient levels at 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100%.

The ORG is a key ICT PDA array that provides the daily price delivery context. When the first-presented FVG aligns with an ORG gradient level, the confluence significantly strengthens the zone.

## Settings

### General Settings

**Only show current day gaps** — toggle (default: on)

When on, all gap drawings reset at 9:30 AM each day. Only the current day's first-presented gaps are visible. Turn off to keep prior days' gaps on the chart for historical reference.

---

**Enable AM gaps** — toggle (default: on)

Toggles detection and drawing of AM session first-presented FVGs. Disable if you only trade the PM session.

---

**Enable PM gaps** — toggle (default: on)

Toggles detection and drawing of PM session first-presented FVGs.

---

**Enable ORG** — toggle (default: on)

Shows the Opening Range Gap and its gradient levels. The ORG forms daily between the prior close and the 9:30 AM open.

## How We Use It

The first-presented FVG is the team's primary reference level for each session. After the 9:30 AM open, the team waits for the first FVG to form in the initial 30-minute window. That zone defines the session's bias — a bullish gap acts as support and a potential buy zone; a bearish gap acts as resistance and a potential sell zone.

The ORG adds daily macro context: when the first-presented AM FVG aligns with one of the ORG gradient levels — particularly the 50% midpoint — the confluence makes it a stronger zone. Price trading down into a bullish first-presented gap that sits at the ORG 50% is a textbook long setup during the AM session. The time and date labels help quickly identify whether a gap formed today or carries over from a prior session.
