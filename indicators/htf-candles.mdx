## Overview

**HTF Candles** displays higher-timeframe candle bodies and wicks directly on your execution chart, positioned to the right of the current price action. Instead of switching timeframes to see the context, you can read the HTF candle structure — open, high, low, close, wick — while staying on the 1-minute or 5-minute chart. This is particularly useful for reading CRT (Candle Range Theory) phases without losing granularity.

The indicator renders up to 10 historical HTF candles plus the current forming one, adds an optional HTF close countdown timer, detects SMT divergences between your instrument and a correlated asset at HTF swing highs and lows, and draws horizontal projection lines for the current HTF candle's OHLC levels across the chart. An info box at the bottom of the screen shows the indicator name, current timeframe, symbol, and date.

The higher timeframe whose candles are rendered. Set to the timeframe you want to analyze while executing on a lower one.

## How We Use It

HTF Candles is used in the same workflow as the CRT indicator — set to 15 minutes on a 1-minute chart to frame the three-candle intraday cycle. The close timer tells the team exactly how far into the current candle they are, which matters for timing entries: entries taken in the first third of the distribution candle have more room to run than entries taken near the close.

The SMT detection at the HTF level is the key signal. When the 15-minute candle sweeps a high or low and the correlated asset does not confirm the new extreme, that SMT on the HTF candle is a high-conviction sign that the sweep is a manipulation — and the team enters on the first 1-minute pullback into an FVG or CISD level.
