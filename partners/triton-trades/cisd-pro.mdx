## Overview

**CISD Model+** is a full-stack entry framework built for the Triton Trades community. It extends the core CISD (Change in State of Delivery) concept into a graded, multi-timeframe system that evaluates each setup against a configurable set of conditions: HTF PDA alignment, FVG confluence, liquidity sweep confirmation, and SMT divergence. Setups that meet more conditions receive higher grades; you can filter to only display setups at or above a minimum grade, keeping the chart clean.

The indicator draws five layers simultaneously: liquidity levels (BSL and SSL), session liquidity references (Asia high/low, London high/low, prior day high/low, prior week high/low), Fair Value Gaps from an automatically or manually selected higher timeframe, CISD lines with potential current-TF detections, and a checklist that tracks which setup conditions are met in real time.

## Settings

### General

**Bias** — `Bullish` | `Bearish` | `Auto`

Filters setups by directional bias. `Auto` shows all setups regardless of direction.

---

**Remove Invalidated Setups** — toggle

When on, setups that have been structurally invalidated are removed from the chart automatically.

---

**Num Setups Displayed** — integer

The maximum number of simultaneous setups shown on the chart.

---

**Min Setup Grade to Display** — grade selector

Filters out setups below this grade. Higher minimum grades show only the strongest confluences.

---

**Include Current TF in HTF** — toggle

When on, the current chart timeframe is included when scanning for HTF CISD formations.

---

**Current TF Liquidity Sweeps** — toggle

Enables liquidity sweep detection on the current timeframe.

---

**Ignore Close CISDs** — toggle

When on, CISD formations that occur very close to the current price are filtered out to avoid noise.

---

**Max CISD Bars** — integer (default: `10`)

The maximum number of bars back that a CISD is considered valid. CISDs older than this are expired.

---

### Liquidity

**Alignment Mode** — `Auto` | `Manual`

In `Auto`, the indicator selects liquidity reference timeframes automatically. In `Manual`, you control them directly.

---

**Show Liquidity** — toggle

Enables drawing of buyside and sellside liquidity level lines.

---

**BSL Color** — color picker

Color of buyside liquidity (swing high) levels.

---

**SSL Color** — color picker

Color of sellside liquidity (swing low) levels.

---

**Show Liquidity Sweeps** — toggle

Marks on the chart when a liquidity level has been swept by price.

---

**Sweep Color / Text** — color pickers

Colors for the liquidity sweep marker and label text.

---

**Session Liquidity** — toggles for `Asia`, `London`, `PDH`, `PDL`, `PWH`, `PWL`

Enables each type of session-based liquidity reference level. PDH/PDL are the prior day high and low; PWH/PWL are the prior week high and low. Each can be independently shown or hidden.

---

### FVG

**Show FVGs** — toggle

Enables Fair Value Gap drawing.

---

**FVG Border** — toggle

Adds a border outline around each FVG box.

---

**FVG Max Per Side** — integer (default: `1`)

The maximum number of FVGs displayed per side (bullish/bearish). Keeping this at 1 surfaces only the most recent relevant gap.

---

**FVG Timeframe Mode** — `Auto` | `Manual`

In `Auto`, the FVG timeframe is selected based on the current chart timeframe. In `Manual`, you set it directly.

---

**FVG Label / Colors** — color pickers

Label and fill colors for bullish and bearish FVG zones.

---

### CISD

**Show CISD** — toggle

Enables CISD line drawing.

---

**Show Potential Current TF CISDs** — toggle

When on, CISD formations that are forming in real time on the current timeframe are displayed before full confirmation. Useful for anticipating the setup.

---

**Disable Current TF Labels** — toggle

Suppresses labels on current-timeframe CISD lines to reduce visual clutter when HTF labels are sufficient.

---

**Detect CISD from SMTs** — toggle

When on, SMT divergence signals at swing points can qualify as CISD events, allowing SMT-confirmed reversals to be treated as CISDs.

---

### SMT

**Enable SMT** — toggle

Enables Smart Money Technique divergence detection.

---

**Comparison Symbol** — symbol

The correlated asset to compare against for SMT detection. Use `NQ1!` when trading ES and `ES1!` when trading NQ.

---

**SMT Style** — color, line style, width

Visual settings for SMT divergence lines and labels.

---

### Alerts

**Enable Alerts** — toggle

Master toggle for all alerts.

---

**Session 1 / 2 / 3** — toggle + session time

Alert windows. Only alerts that fire within an enabled session window are delivered.

---

Alert toggles and messages are provided for CISD formation, setup confirmation, and setup invalidation events.

## How We Use It

CISD Model+ is Triton Trades' primary execution tool. The grading system allows the community to filter for only A+ or B+ setups by setting a minimum grade, eliminating the marginal setups that require more discretionary evaluation. The session liquidity levels (Asia high/low, London high/low, PDH/PDL, PWH/PWL) serve as the draw targets, and the FVG + CISD combination defines the entry. The checklist table runs continuously during the session — when all five conditions are lit up simultaneously, that's the highest-conviction setup. The `Detect CISD from SMTs` toggle is particularly useful at session extremes where an SMT divergence at a sweep coincides with a structural shift.
